Scuba Diving Courses
Scuba diving is an underwater swimming activity involving the use of self-contained underwater breathing apparatus. Divers have to carry their own source of breathing gas, usually compressed air, affording them greater independence and movement than surface-supplied divers, and more time underwater than free divers. PADI Scuba Diver rating may be the best option for you if you need to become a certified diver quickly, especially if you want to dive mostly with a dive guide. If obtaining an Open Water Diver certification is your ultimate objective, this course serves as an intermediate step toward that goal. Real close. You will discover creatures that you might be able to see only on television or the big screen.

Deep Diving Courses
The main reason we divers have to dive deep is to see things that you can't see at shallow waters. It's quite common for well preserved wrecks to be found in deeper water, as the greater depth means less exposure to surface surge. You will also find that different marine life exists at different depths. The deep's allure. When scuba diving, investigating deeper dive spots has a certain allure and mystique. A wreck may occasionally draw you down to a depth of 18 meters (60 feet), whereas on wall dives it may be a huge fan or sponge. Whatever it may be, you should enroll in the PADI Deep Diver Specialty course if you want to dive with confidence down to 40 meters/130 feet.
Duration : 3 days                         Maximum depth : 50 meters
Minimum age : 15 years old        Equipment : included
Price : $400
